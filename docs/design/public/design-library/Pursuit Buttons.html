Component exploration · Buttons

Eight ways Pursuit could click.

Same three states across each direction (primary · ghost · accent + size variants), so you're comparing voice, not coverage. The current direction is #1; the rest reach in different directions — more editorial, more brutalist, more refined. Hover them.

Direction 01

Currently shipping

Pill · soft

Fully-rounded capsule with a quiet purple-tinted shadow. Friendly, app-native, what's in the mobile UI kit today.

Best for

Everyday CTAs. Booking, saving, filling forms.

Primary
Ghost

Direction 02

More editorial

Sharp · editorial

Squared 4px corners, all-caps tracking. Reads like a magazine sidebar — confident, opinionated, less app-feeling.

Best for

Editor's Pick CTAs, "Read the brief", page-level hero actions. Calmer surfaces.

Primary
Ghost

Direction 03

Bolder

Block · drop-shadow

Solid border + hard offset shadow, presses down to flat on click. Risky on a calm canvas — strongest on featured tiles, ticket bars, and weekend brief teasers.

Best for

One marquee CTA per page. Don't use for routine actions.

Primary
Ghost

Direction 04

Editorial · minimal

Underline · "read more"

No box, no shadow — just type and a baseline rule. Arrow nudges right on hover. Reads as a magazine "continue" link.

Best for

Secondary CTAs inside editorial blocks. "Read the full pick", "See the brief", "View 12 more".

In context

"Half the city will tell you Wednesdays at the Alchemist are a tourist trap. They're wrong."

Sizes

Direction 05

Premium · refined

Pill · circular node

Capsule with an inset arrow circle. The arrow node flips white on hover and the pill widens slightly — feels considered, premium, hospitality-grade.

Best for

Booking CTAs on premium event detail pages. Membership upsells. "Build the plan".

Primary
Ghost

Direction 06

Functional · with detail

Two-tone · split fill

A label and a suffix in different colors, sharing one pill. Lets you put the price (or count) right on the CTA without it feeling like an afterthought.

Best for

Checkout CTAs ("Pay · KES 12,645"), filter pills with counts, anywhere price/quantity is part of the action.

Primary
Filters

Direction 07

Tactile

Material · soft inset

Subtle inner highlight + offset shadow makes the button feel pressable; presses physically down on click. Quiet but unmistakably an interactive surface.

Best for

Form submits, settings, quantity steppers, M-Pesa confirmation — places that should feel like real buttons.

Primary
Quiet

Direction 08

Utility · refined

Hairline · 36px

A pared-back utility button — small, hairline border, no shadow. Doesn't compete with imagery or editorial type. Pairs with one of the bolder directions above as the primary.

Best for

Filters, sort pills, "View all", icon-buttons, settings rows. The supporting cast, never the lead.

My pairing recommendation

Primary: stick with #1 (Pill · soft) or upgrade to #5 (Pill · circular node) for marquee booking CTAs. Secondary: #4 (Underline) inside editorial blocks — Pursuit Says, briefs, "view all". Utility: #8 (Hairline) for filters, sort, settings. Reach for #2 only on dedicated editorial pages (organizer profile, brief archive); skip #3 unless one marquee CTA per surface is what you want.
